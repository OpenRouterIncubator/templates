# Sequencing

A sequence is a small number of *useful* touches, each adding something new —
not the same ask repeated until they block you. Every message is still a draft
for a human to review and send (`draft-outreach`); this skill plans the shape,
spacing, and stop conditions.

## Default shape (adjust to context)

1. **Touch 1 — the personalized open.** The strongest, most specific message,
   built from the research card. One clear, low-friction ask.
2. **Touch 2 (after ~3–4 business days) — add value, don't nag.** A relevant
   resource, a second angle, or a genuinely new observation. Never "just bumping
   this to the top of your inbox."
3. **Touch 3 (after ~5–7 days) — the respectful close.** Acknowledge they may not
   be interested, make it easy to say no, leave the door open. This is the last
   unsolicited touch.

Three good touches beat seven mediocre ones. Stop at the close unless they
engage.

## Stop immediately when

- They reply asking to stop, or opt out in any form — honor it permanently.
- They reply with disinterest ("not a fit", "no budget") — close gracefully, no
  rebuttal sequence.
- A quiet signal of annoyance appears, or the trigger that justified outreach is
  clearly gone.
- They engage positively — switch from sequence to a real conversation; stop the
  automation.

## Spacing and honesty

- Give real gaps between touches; same-day or next-day follow-ups read as
  pressure.
- Never imply a prior conversation that didn't happen ("following up on our
  chat") if there was none.
- Track touches so you never exceed the planned count or double-send. When in
  doubt, send less.
