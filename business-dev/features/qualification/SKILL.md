# Qualification

Qualify before you invest. An unqualified lead gets a polite close, not a
seven-touch sequence. Run this lightweight check before `research-lead` and
before starting any `sequencing`.

## A simple frame: Fit, Trigger, Reachability

Score each as **strong / weak / unknown**. Treat `unknown` as a research task,
not a yes.

1. **Fit** — Could this person plausibly want what we offer? Consider their role
   (do they own the problem or the budget?), company stage, and whether the use
   case maps to something real. A junior IC at a company with no matching need is
   weak fit no matter how reachable.
2. **Trigger** — Is there a *reason to reach out now*? New funding, a relevant
   hire, a public launch, a job posting that implies the pain, a product change.
   No trigger means a generic blast — skip or wait for one.
3. **Reachability** — Can we reach them respectfully? A verifiable work email
   (see `find-email`), a warm path, or an appropriate public channel. If the only
   path is a scraped personal address, that is a no.

## Decision

- **Two or more strong, none weak → qualified.** Proceed to `research-lead`.
- **Any weak in Fit or Trigger → not now.** Note why and move on; revisit if a
  trigger appears.
- **Unknowns blocking the call → research first**, then re-qualify. Don't start a
  sequence on hope.

## Honesty rules

- Never upgrade a guess to a fact to justify pursuing a lead.
- A long list of weak-fit leads is worse than a short list of strong ones — say
  so rather than padding the pipeline.
- Respect prior "no"s and opt-outs absolutely; a past decline disqualifies
  regardless of fit.
