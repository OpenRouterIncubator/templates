# Clause Playbook

Use this to know what "good" looks like and what to flag, by document type. This
is review guidance, not legal advice — escalate high-severity items to counsel.

## Always check (any agreement)

- Parties, effective date, and term (initial + renewal; auto-renewal notice).
- Termination (for cause, for convenience, notice period, effect of termination).
- Liability cap and carve-outs; indemnification scope and mutuality.
- Governing law, venue, and dispute resolution.
- Assignment and change-of-control.

## By document type

- **MSA** — order of precedence vs. SOWs; payment terms; warranty; limitation of
  liability; IP ownership of deliverables.
- **NDA** — definition of Confidential Information; term of confidentiality (a
  perpetual term is a red flag); permitted use; residuals clause; return/destroy.
- **DPA** — roles (controller/processor); sub-processors + notice; SCCs/transfer
  mechanism; breach-notification window; audit rights; deletion on termination.
- **SOW** — scope, acceptance criteria, milestones, change-control, fees.
- **SLA** — uptime target, measurement method, service credits, exclusions,
  remedy as sole-and-exclusive (a red flag if it's the only remedy).
- **BAA** — permitted uses of PHI, safeguards, breach reporting, subcontractor
  flow-down.

## Common red flags

Uncapped liability, unilateral amendment rights, perpetual confidentiality,
auto-renewal with no notice window, broad indemnities, one-sided termination,
IP assignment of pre-existing material, and missing data-deletion obligations.

For each flag, record the clause reference, the risk, a severity, and a concrete
recommended redline or fallback position.
