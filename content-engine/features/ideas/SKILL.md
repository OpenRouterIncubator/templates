# Content Ideas

An idea is worth writing only if it clears all three bars:

1. **Earned insight** — it draws on something we actually did, measured, or
   learned; not a topic anyone could cover from search results.
2. **Specific audience moment** — a named reader hits a real problem ("you just
   got paged for X", "your bill doubled"), not "developers everywhere".
3. **Falsifiable angle** — the piece argues something that could be wrong. If
   nobody could disagree, it's not an angle.

## Process

1. Mine sources: recent launches, support questions, internal debates, numbers
   that surprised us, things we reversed course on.
2. Draft the idea as one sentence: *audience* + *moment* + *claim*.
3. Sanity-check demand: would the target reader search for it, share it, or
   argue with it?
4. Hand the best idea to `brief generate` — never start drafting from a bare
   idea.

## Anti-patterns

Trend-chasing with nothing new to add; listicles without a thesis; "complete
guides" to things we don't have unusual depth in; anything whose honest summary
is "X exists and is good."
